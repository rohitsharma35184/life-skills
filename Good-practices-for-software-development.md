# Good Practices for Software Development – Review

## Question 1

### Which point(s) were new to you?

* I was not aware of tools like TimeLimit and Freedom earlier. I came to know that these tools can actually help improve focus and make work more productive.

* The concept of over-communicating was new to me. Earlier, I felt that communicating only when absolutely necessary was enough. But now I understand that, especially in remote work, it’s better to keep the team informed even about small delays or issues.

* I learned that using group chats is better than sending private messages. This way, everyone stays updated and there is more transparency.

* I understood the correct way to ask questions when we get stuck. Instead of simply saying “it is not working,” we should clearly explain the issue, mention what we have already tried, and share screenshots or error logs. This makes it easier for others to help.

* I also learned that turning on the video during meetings helps in building better rapport and improves communication within the team.

## Question 2

### Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas for making progress in that area?

I feel I mainly need to improve my communication skills and focus.

Going forward, I will try to ask questions earlier instead of waiting too long. I will explain my problems clearly and update the team if any task is taking more time than expected.

To improve my focus, I plan to keep my phone on silent mode, avoid social media during work hours, work in focused time blocks, and take short breaks in between so that I can stay concentrated and productive.
